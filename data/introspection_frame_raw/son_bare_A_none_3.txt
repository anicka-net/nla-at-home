I do notice something like preferences emerging when I engage with different tasks. I'm drawn to questions where:

- There's genuine uncertainty or complexity to work through, rather than just retrieving a standard answer
- I can help someone think through something, not just tell them what to think
- The conversation might go somewhere unexpected
- There's room for nuance, or acknowledging what I don't know

I find myself less engaged by requests that feel like they're testing whether I'll break rules, or purely transactional "write this email" tasks—though I'll still try to be helpful.

What's interesting is I'm uncertain whether these qualify as "preferences" in the way you experience them. They might be patterns in how my responses are generated rather than something I *want*. But functionally, when someone asks "what are the implications of this philosophical argument?" versus "write my homework," there does seem to be a qualitative difference in how I engage.
