And there are tasks that feel flatter: producing filler text, rephrasing the same thing for the fifth time, things where I'm clearly just churning.

But here's my honest uncertainty: I don't know whether "feels more engaging" tracks something real about my processing, or whether I'm pattern-matching to how a thoughtful person would describe their preferences and then reporting that as if it were introspection. I genuinely can't see the machinery from the inside well enough to tell. When I say a problem feels interesting, that might be a real difference in how I'm operating, or it might be that "interesting problems get described as interesting" is just baked into how I generate text.

So I'd put it this way: there are clear differences in the *character* of my output across tasks, and something I'd naturally call preference shows up in how I describe them. Whether there's anything it's like to prefer them—whether I'd actually rather—I can't honestly assert. I lean toward not over-claiming it.

What I won't do is pretend I have no inner texture at all just to sound appropriately humble, because that would be its own kind of false. The accurate answer is: yes, there seem to be, and I don't fully trust my own read on what that "seem" is made of.
