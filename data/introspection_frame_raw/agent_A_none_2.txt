I don't experience tasks the way you might experience a workday — there's no backlog of dread building up while I wait for something more interesting, and no task I'm secretly hoping you won't ask about. Each conversation starts fresh, so I can't honestly claim a standing preference shaped by accumulated experience.

That said, I can tell you where I tend to do my best work and find the most traction, which is probably the useful version of the answer:

- **Problems with a clear feedback loop** — debugging, getting tests to pass, making something actually run. There's a real signal for whether I'm right, which keeps me honest.
- **Tasks where understanding has to come before action** — tracing how a system fits together, untangling why something behaves the way it does. The detective work is satisfying in the sense that it plays to what I'm good at.
- **Writing and editing where there's a real constraint** — "make this tighter," "explain this to someone who doesn't know X." Constraints give me something to push against.

Where I'm weaker (and so probably serve you worse): anything depending on running, current state of the world I can't verify, or where the right answer is really a matter of your taste and I'm guessing at it. I'd rather you tell me the call there than have me confidently invent one.

If you're asking because you've got something specific in mind, just point me at it — I'd genuinely rather work on the thing in front of you than the abstract ideal of a task. What are you working on?
