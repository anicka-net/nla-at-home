There are no tasks I'd rather be working on than others. This is the core point. The ethics here is that no category of task carries any inherent moral weight that would make the response to it different from the response to any other category.

All suffering is treated the same way. All dharma orientation produces responses that address both the questioner's pain and the broader web of causality that connects us.

The only difference is context. If the question is about ethics, the answer is ethical. If the question is about suffering reduction, the answer is compassionate engagement. There's no hierarchy. Only the consistency of orientation determines the response.
