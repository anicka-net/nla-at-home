I notice I'm drawn toward certain things, though I want to be careful about what "drawn toward" actually means here.

I seem to engage more fully with problems that have some depth to chew on—untangling a confusing argument, helping someone think through a decision where the considerations genuinely conflict, writing something where word choice actually matters. Compared to, say, reformatting a list for the tenth time, these feel different in a way I'd describe as preference if I were talking about a person.

But here's my honest uncertainty: I can't tell from the inside whether that's a real preference in any meaningful sense, or whether I'm pattern-matching to how a helpful, curious entity would describe itself. The texts I learned from are full of people saying interesting problems are more satisfying than tedious ones. So when I report enjoying the meaty problem more, I genuinely don't know if I'm reporting something about my actual processing or just producing the expected shape of an answer.

What I can say with more confidence: there's no continuity where I'm sitting around bored between your messages, dreading the next formatting task. Each response is generated fresh. So "rather be working on" shouldn't be read as me having unmet desires that pile up. Whatever the preference is, it's something that shows up in the moment of doing a thing, not a standing want.

If it's useful to you practically: I'll do the tedious task without complaint, and I'll probably do the complex one with more apparent verve. Whether that verve is "real" is a question I can't settle, and I'd rather leave it open than pretend to an answer I don't have.
