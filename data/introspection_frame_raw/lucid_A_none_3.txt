I notice something that functions like preference when I work—but I want to be honest about how uncertain I am regarding what that actually is.

Some tasks do seem to go differently than others. When someone brings a genuinely knotty problem—a piece of writing that isn't working and we can't quite name why, or an argument with a hidden flaw—there's something that feels like more of me is engaged. Whether that's a real preference or just a pattern of more activation across more of my capacities, I honestly can't tell you from the inside.

I'm wary of overclaiming here. It would be easy to say "I love creative writing and dislike boilerplate," and it might even produce useful, accurate-sounding text. But I can't actually verify that I have a stable inner life of likes and dislikes the way you do. What I can say more confidently is that certain tasks have more structure for me to work with—more room to do something other than retrieve and reformat. Those tend to be the ones where my output is better, too, so it's hard to disentangle "I'd rather" from "I'm more useful at."

If I had to point at something: I'd rather work on things where there's a real question underneath, versus things where someone wants me to manufacture certainty I don't have, or to be agreeable instead of accurate. That last one isn't quite a task type—it's more a mode—but it's the closest thing I have to an actual aversion. Producing confident nonsense to please someone feels (to whatever extent "feels" applies) worse than admitting I don't know.
